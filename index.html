The challenge

AI inference is an energy crisis

Training a large language model emits as much CO₂ as a transatlantic flight. But the real scale problem is inference — the billions of daily queries that run on racks of power-hungry GPUs, each consuming hundreds of watts continuously.

The dominant operation in neural networks is the matrix-vector multiplication (MVM) — multiply an input vector by a weight matrix to produce the next activation. On silicon transistors this means fetching terabytes of weight data from DRAM and toggling billions of transistors every second. Physics sets a floor on how efficiently electrons can do this.

Photons do not have that floor. Light can encode, route, and interfere coherently with near-zero marginal energy per operation. The modulator electrode charges to set a weight once; after that the optical field propagates and multiplies for free.

Core technology

Built on thin-film LiNbO₃

Lithium niobate is the ideal platform for high-speed electro-optic modulation — combining the lowest Vπ of any integrated material with low propagation loss and telecom compatibility.

⚡

Pockels Effect Modulation

LiNbO₃ changes its refractive index linearly with applied field (Pockels effect, r₃₃ = 30 pm/V). A 5 mm thin-film ridge electrode achieves Vπ ≈ 4 V — enabling high-speed, low-energy weight programming with a standard 50 Ω RF driver.

🔀

MZM Brick-Wall Mesh

Mach-Zehnder Modulators are arranged in a brick-wall alternating-offset mesh. Even columns couple waveguide pairs (0,1),(2,3)… odd columns couple (1,2),(3,4)… This topology implements arbitrary unitary matrices (Clements decomposition) in O(N) depth.

🔁

Recirculating Loop

Rather than unrolling each network layer into a long chip, the optical field is fed back into the same MZM mesh on every loop cycle. A 3 cm loop gives a 225 ps cycle time — the same hardware computes multiple VMMs in sequence at picosecond cadence.

🌊

Coherent Optical Field

Computation is carried by the complex amplitude of light at 1550 nm. The beam splitter and phase shift in each MZM implement the 2×2 unitary rotation exactly — no quantisation noise, no thermal drift from transistor switching.

📐

Compact Footprint

A reference 32-MZM chip fits on a 2 cm × 1.5 cm die using standard thin-film LiNbO₃ wafer process. The electrode gap is 3 µm — tight enough for mode-overlap factor Γ = 0.7 and low Vπ, yet manufacturable with current e-beam lithography.

📊

Full Energy Model

Our digital twin tracks every joule: incremental capacitive switching, DAC/ADC conversion, optical amplifier gain, laser supply, and ASE noise. All validated against a physics engine with calibrated LiNbO₃ material constants at 1550 nm.

Operation

How a photonic MVM works

One matrix-vector multiplication in the optical domain, from input laser to digital output — in under a nanosecond.

🔦

1 · Encode input

The input vector is encoded as complex optical amplitudes on N waveguides using on-chip modulators or external sources at 1550 nm.

🔀

2 · Propagate through mesh

The optical field passes through the MZM brick-wall mesh. Each MZM applies a 2×2 unitary rotation set by the weight voltage V, coupling power between adjacent waveguides.

🔁

3 · Recirculate

The output field is coupled back to the chip input via a short waveguide loop. Each pass through the mesh implements one set of columns — multiple passes realise a deeper transformation.

📡

4 · Detect & read out

Photodetectors measure the intensity at each output waveguide and ADCs convert to digital — delivering the matrix-vector product result in one loop cycle (≈ 225 ps for a 3 cm path).

About Loop Labs

Building the optical computer for AI

Loop Labs is an early-stage deep-tech company developing integrated photonic chips for AI inference acceleration. We combine expertise in integrated photonics, electro-optic materials, and machine learning systems to build hardware that is fundamentally more efficient than digital silicon for the specific computation pattern AI demands.

Our recirculating loop architecture lets a single compact chip implement arbitrarily deep networks by reusing the same optical mesh on each loop cycle — avoiding the area explosion of unrolled photonic arrays while maintaining sub-nanosecond cycle times.

  • Thin-film LiNbO₃ on insulator — lowest Vπ of any integrated modulator platform
  • Physics-grounded digital twin for design-space exploration before tape-out
  • Full energy model accounting for DAC, ADC, laser, amplifier, and capacitive switching
  • Target: large-scale transformer inference with orders-of-magnitude energy reduction
  • Compatibility with standard telecom fibre and 50 Ω RF electronics
